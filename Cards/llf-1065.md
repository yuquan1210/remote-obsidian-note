After investigation, we conclude that the reason for the two cases are the same. It is because the succeeding lead is sent right after the first lead (both around 30 seconds).

CDP upsert lead is asynchronous process, meaning that even though we received an upsert success response, it does not guarantee the lead has been written into their table. So when the next lead came and ALA check if there is any lead associated with the customer, no lead found in the table and pass duplicaiton check and proceeds to lead allocation to franchise logic.

To conclude, consumer submitting new leads within a very short time (<= 30 seconds) will likely cause this issue

In Emma case, we see that for the second lead, CDP returned no lead associated with this customer at time 23:19:56.070. The first lead was saved into their datastore at time 23:19:57.610

Proposed solutions:

Considering that the root cause is CDP save is asynchronous, a possible solution is in our duplication check after checking ds lead table, we also check ds lead archive table for recently 'allocated' leads within recent 5 minutes for example, and use that to decide if there is any lead just created under this customer. Given that our save to ds lead archive function is synchronous, data saved into the table is immediately readable and so the problem should not occur.


We investigated this card about the same customer had duplicate leads allocated to franchises.
Root cause: Both cases were caused by the consumer submitting a second lead within ~30 seconds of the first. ALA's duplication check calls CDP to verify whether any lead already exists for the customer. However, CDP's upsert operation is asynchronous. In both cases, the second lead's CDP check returned no existing leads because the first lead had not yet been persisted (the gap was ~1.5–2 seconds).

As a result, the second lead passed the duplication check and was allocated to a franchise. A potential solution idea is documented in the git issue (#432) in case we need to fix it.
