**Relation between `mclead.com.au` and `mc-ala`**
- In Akamai Terraform, `mclead.com.au` is explicitly described as **“the primary hostname for MC-ALA shortened URLs”**.
- The traffic flow in preprod/prod is roughly:  
    `mortgage-choice-adapter → https(s)://preprod.mclead.com.au/allocate_lead?... → Akamai → ala-preprod.mortgagechoice.com.au/allocate_lead → GCP LB → Cloud Function / backend in mc-ala`.
- The **URL-shortener design and implementation live in the `mc-ala` repo**, with `mclead.com.au` chosen as the public domain that those short links use.

So:
- **`mc-ala` is the service** that generates and resolves the lead-acceptance short URLs.
- **`mclead.com.au` is the Akamai front door / hostname for those short URLs**, pointing into `mc-ala`’s “short_url_redirector`/`allocate_lead` path.

The “conversion” is:
- **Akamai**: from `mclead.com.au` to the **ALA origin host** (`ala-*.mortgagechoice.com.au`).
- **Short-URL redirector** in **`mc-ala`**: from short URL to the **real long acceptance URL**.


why design mc-ala in place of mclead.com.au
- you _could_ technically hang this off `mortgagechoice.com.au`, but that would tie lead-acceptance to a much larger, more complex surface area (website infra, SEO, content, WAF rules, bug-bounty surface, etc.). Using `mclead.com.au` keeps the **lead short-URL flow small, owned by Lead Lords/MC-ALA, and safer to operate independently**

what ssl certificate is for?
- An SSL/TLS certificate does two main things:
- Proves identity (authentication)
	- It’s a digital ID issued by a trusted Certificate Authority that says “this public key really belongs to mclead.com.au (or whatever domain).”
	- Your browser checks the cert to be sure it’s talking to the real site, not an impostor.
- Enables encryption (confidentiality + integrity)
	- It’s used during the HTTPS handshake to establish an encrypted channel between browser and server.
	- This protects data (logins, personal info, etc.) from being read or modified by anyone on the network (e.g. Wi-Fi snoopers, proxies, etc.).
